The Product

The Apprentice's Kit brings this fantasy to life through a spellcasting bundle designed for tabletop gamers, collectors, and occult enthusiasts alike.

  • A handcrafted spellbook filled with original incantations, ritual ingredients, and mystical illustrations; with TTRPG-ready roleplay mechanics woven into each spell.
  • A custom-designed set of metal runic dice, dubbed The Dice of Destiny; used to generate randomized spellcasting results and combos.
  • A leather pouch etched with cult iconography; designed to hold your dice in arcane style.

What I Did

I co-wrote the spellbook with Tess Goodwin, a writer with deep knowledge of magical systems. I structured the content, added clarity, and wrote all the roleplaying mechanics to make each spell playable and game-friendly.

I designed the metal dice in AutoCAD and developed spec sheets for both the dice and all packaging elements. I also created the Cult of Mathilda logo and managed all formatting and layout for printing.

We worked with four international vendors:

  • Two book printers; one for the spellbook, and another for the lore booklet
  • A dice casting manufacturer for the runic metal dice
  • A leather goods supplier for the pouch

I coordinated final assembly by having all components shipped to one central vendor for packaging and fulfillment. Our launch at San Diego Comic-Con was locked in stone, so we had to move fast and smart. To hit the deadline, I made the call to split printing between two separate book vendors; one for the spellbook and another for the storybook; so they could run in parallel and shave days off production. It added a bit of complexity, but it bought us the time we needed without compromising print quality.

The dice were another beast entirely. I worked directly with a metal casting vendor overseas, and after some back-and-forth negotiations, I secured a large MOQ discount that let us scale up the dice run for almost half the original price. That savings gave us breathing room in the budget to hire layout help and pay for faster shipping, which made the whole package come together just in time.

Every piece; the books, the dice, the pouch; was produced by a different manufacturer. I coordinated the logistics so everything landed at one central hub for packaging, QC, and distribution. It was a classic example of creative production meets logistical challenge, and we pulled it off on time.
